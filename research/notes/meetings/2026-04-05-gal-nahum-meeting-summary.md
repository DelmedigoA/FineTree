# Meeting Summary: Gal Nahum

## Meeting Details

- **Date:** April 5, 2026
- **Time:** 21:00
- **Participant:** Gal Nahum
- **Role:** Accountant in training
- **Purpose:** Validate the product direction, understand the relevance of accounting standards, and clarify the target audience.

## Summary

This meeting focused on validating the current product direction and sharpening the practical value the product should deliver. The main takeaway was that the product should avoid going deep into accounting definitions, formal standards interpretation, and the kinds of technical complexity that mainly serve accountants. Instead, the product should provide value by helping users understand how to read financial statements in a practical and accessible way.

Gal's input helped clarify that the primary audience should not be accountants. A stronger direction is to orient the product toward tax-facing users, especially users connected to the Tax Authority and similar use cases. In that framing, accounting standards remain relevant as background knowledge, but they should not become the center of the user experience.

## Key Discussion Points

- IFRS and IAS 2 inventory were discussed as background context, not as the main focus of the product.
- The product should not attempt to teach deep accounting definitions or recreate the complexity of professional accounting work.
- The core user value should be understanding and reading financial statements, not preparing or constructing them.
- Accountants are not the primary target audience for this product direction.
- A more relevant audience is tax-facing users, with particular relevance to the Tax Authority.
- Deep knowledge of IFRS, standards, and formal reporting logic belongs to professional accounting training and academic study, not to the product's main value proposition.

## Conclusions

- Keep accounting standards in the background as supporting context only.
- Do not position the product as a tool for building financial statements.
- Position the product as a tool that helps users read and interpret financial statements.
- Reduce emphasis on accountants as the core user group.
- Increase emphasis on tax-facing users and Tax Authority-related use cases.
